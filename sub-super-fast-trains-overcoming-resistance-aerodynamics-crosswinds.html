Crosswinds - Don't Roll Over!

 
click here click here

High winds can be dangerous for super-fast trains. Head-on winds can increase drag, but side-on winds, called 'crosswinds', could turn the whole train over!

This works just like pressure drag. As the crosswind comes across the nose of the train, it separates on the side facing away from the wind. So you have low pressure on that side, while there's high pressure on the side facing the wind.

What you end up with is a side-force on the train. This pushes close to the roof, and this means something then happens around the wheels... what is it?

Well, it's called a rolling 'moment'. A 'moment' is when a force, like the wind, acts like a lever around a certain spot. If the rolling moment is large enough around the place where the wheel sits on the rail, the train can roll over! But super-fast trains are designed to have low side-force and balance their weight to make sure they can't roll over!
