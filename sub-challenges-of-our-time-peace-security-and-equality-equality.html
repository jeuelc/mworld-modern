Many of the world's religions were founded on the belief that everyone should be treated equally, whether they are male or female, able bodied or disabled, rich or poor, healthy or ill.

Equality means people should have the same dignity, freedom, rights and respect no matter what they look like, how old they are, where they come from, what they believe in, who their life partner is, or whether they are male or female. When people are not treated equally in this way we call this 'discrimination'.

Many countries have improved levels of equality over the past 100 years. But there is still more to do. There are still many unofficial forms of discrimination in many countries, and there are also many official forms of discrimination in some countries. For example, girls do not have the same rights to go to school as boys in some parts of the world.
