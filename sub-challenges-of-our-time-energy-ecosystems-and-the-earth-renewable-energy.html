Renewable Energy

Fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas form over millions of years. So if we used all the Earth's current reserves of fossil fuels, we would have to wait a long time before we could find any more. There would be no more petrol for your car, plane or bus!

But fortunately there are other sources of energy that will never run out, or they won't run out for many millions of years. We call this 'renewable energy'. The sun's rays, for example, can be used to create electricity by heating solar panels. The power of wind and ocean waves, or steam created by pouring water on hot rocks deep in the Earth, can be used to turn turbines, creating electricity.

These sources of energy are not just renewable, they also produce far less pollution than fossil fuels. But they're not perfect; often they cost more than fossil fuels. And it's hard to get the energy created by an ocean wave into the engine of your car!

But these sources of energy are very promising and could help to combat climate change.
