Food For Nine Billion?

There are more than seven billion people in the world today. By 2040 the world population will be more than eight billion people, and it might reach nine billion by 2050. Will there be enough food, water and other resources for nine billion people?

If we have nearly a billion hungry people today, what will happen in 2040? Well, it's not all bad news. New technology and farming methods enable farmers to increase the amount of food they can produce. More efficient transport and markets can reduce the amount of food that is wasted. If the incomes of people living in poverty rise, then they have more money available to buy food.

Some people have done studies that suggest the world could produce enough food for nine or ten billion people or more. But this depends on a range of factors such as what farmers use their land for, the health of their crops, how much food is wasted, the efficiency of markets, and what people choose to eat.
