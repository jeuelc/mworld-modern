Inflation

Inflation is when prices keep going up and up. It happens when people are buying lots of stuff because central banks are printing too much money. Inflation means that the wages that you earn will buy less than they did before. This makes it harder to buy the same things you are used to buying!

When inflation happens, people feel uncertain about the future and they become less likely to invest money. This leads to a reduction in the amount of man-made aids such as machines and equipment being bought, making workers less efficient. This leads to lower economic growth over time.

Our governments usually try to keep inflation low, at around 4%, but sometimes food shortages or economic issues can make this difficult. In 1993 inflation in the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia rose to 331,000,000%. Around this time their largest bank notes had a value of 500 billion dinars!
