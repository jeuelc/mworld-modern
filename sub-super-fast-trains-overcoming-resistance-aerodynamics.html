Aerodynamics

Aerodynamics is how the train interacts with the air around it. Resistance due to air (aerodynamic resistance) is also known as drag. A super-fast train experiences two types of drag: 'pressure drag' and 'skin friction'.

Pressure drag is the resistance to pushing the train's shape through the air, so the nose, tail and body shape are really important! (this is why pressure drag is also known as 'form drag') Pointy trains are easier to push through the air than a square one would be!

Skin friction is the resistance the train's surface experiences as it slides through the air.Extra aerodynamic effects that are also important are how the train reacts to gusts of wind, and how the train can causes its own wind gusts and pressure waves.
