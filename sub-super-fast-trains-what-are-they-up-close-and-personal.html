Super-fast trains need some special parts to work. A full train is around 200 metres long and is divided into four carriages with gaps in between. These gaps allow the train to bend so that it can turn corners.

Super-fast trains are also 'bi-directional': they dont turn around to travel in the other direction. This means that going one way, one end is the nose, but going the other, the same end becomes the tail!

Some parts have pretty weird names. Pantographs stick out of the roof of the train and connect it to the overhead power lines to provide electricity to the motors. Bogies connect the train to the rails. A train has two bogies per carriage and four wheels per bogie; can you figure out how many wheels a super-fast train has?
