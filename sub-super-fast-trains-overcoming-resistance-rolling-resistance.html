Rolling Resistance

 
click here click here

Rolling resistance happens between the train's wheels and the track.

Things that can affect rolling resistance are what the wheels are made of, what the track is made of, and the weight of the train. Usually softer material and a heavier vehicle means more rolling resistance (try riding a bike through sand it's hard work!)

Because super-fast trains have steel wheels that run on steel rails, they have the lowest rolling resistance of all wheeled vehicles! The main thing that causes rolling resistance in super-fast trains is when the wheels slip over the rails. Any energy wasted in causing vibrations of the track also adds to the resistance.
