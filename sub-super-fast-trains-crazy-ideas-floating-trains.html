Floating Trains

Using magnets like in MagLev trains isn't the only way to float a train above its rails.

Hover trains float above the rail by sitting on an air cushion created by fans and kept in place by rubber skirts underneath the train.Another way is adding short wings to a train to take advantage of 'ground effect'. The presence of the ground cushions the wing, increasing the pressure on the underside surface. So you get more lift closer to the ground than up in the air.

For a method to be successful it has to be efficient (how much energy does it need and how much does it save?) and it can't be too costly to engineer, or design and build. Can you think of a cool way to float a train and remove rolling resistance?
