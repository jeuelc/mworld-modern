Have you ever been really excited to see an episode of your favourite show? Maybe you even ate dinner a little early, or made sure that you were home so that you didn't miss it! Sometimes we even get a big group of friends together so that we can watch the events together as they happen.

Before mass media existed our schedules were organised by events like the rising and setting of the sun, religious ceremonies or the start and finish of the working day.

With the rise of media, radio and TV in particular, many people began to organise their lives around these schedules instead. Television and radio networks noticed this happening and tried to organise their own schedules they wanted to make sure that as many people as possible were tuning in at the same time, like Saturday nights.

So reality TV can get lots of people doing the same thing at the same time. Can you think why TV networks might want to do this? How does television scheduling influence your life?
