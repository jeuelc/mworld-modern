Food, Water and Medicine

In the year 2000, a group of world leaders came together to set goals for a better future. We call these the Millennium Development Goals. Goal number one was to reduce by half the percentage of people in the world who live in extreme poverty and hunger. Extreme poverty means having an income of only $1.25 per day. How much food do you think you could buy with $1.25?

This goal has been achieved, a great result but that still leaves the other half. More than a billion people around the world still live in extreme poverty, and nearly 900 million people around the world don't have enough food to eat.

Water is just as important as food. You need it to drink, to go to the toilet, and to wash yourself. Likewise, you need medicine to stop you from getting sick, or to get healthy again if you do get sick. Many people don't have access to clean water or medicine.
