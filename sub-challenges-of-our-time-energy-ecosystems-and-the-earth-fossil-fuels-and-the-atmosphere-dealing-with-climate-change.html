The Earth's climate has changed many times over thousands and millions of years. Sometimes the Earth was much hotter than it is today, at other times it was much colder.

Now human activity such as the burning of fossil fuels is making the Earth warmer, and this might cause serious problems. Rising oceans might swamp coastal communities. We might not be able to grow enough food to feed everybody.

Many people are worried by this and want to take action to prevent this from happening, for example by reducing the amount of energy we use and by developing new, clean sources of power.

These actions might save lives and money in the long term, but might cost a lot in the short term. Many people are worried about the costs, and the governments of the world have not yet agreed who should pay for these costs.
