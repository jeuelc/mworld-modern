What would you do if an extra few thousand people arrived in your city every day looking for homes and work? There are lots of challenges and problems facing the people who are in charge of running megacities.

One big problem is providing a house to everybody in such a fast-growing city. The governments of megacities simply can't keep up with all that growth, and when people build their own housing on whatever land they can find, that creates new problems. Sometimes people build a little shelter on a steep slope that washes away in heavy rain. Or too close to rivers and streams that get polluted from so many people living nearby. And how can all these people move around the city?

Megacity mayors have to try to think about all these challenges in fast-growing megacities.
