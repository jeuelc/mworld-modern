What Is Money?

The money you see is mostly paper notes or coins. But money is also in the form of bank deposits. You know how much you have because the bank gives you a receipt, called a 'statement'.

Money is convenient in four important ways. First, it's something you can easily use to make a payment in exchange for goods or services. Throughout history salt, cattle, furs, tobacco and arrowheads have been used as money. Imagine trying to fit cows in your money box!

Second, money is a thing of value to people, businesses and governments and it is how you store your wealth. This is why people often call it a 'store of value'. Third, money is an easy way to measure what something is worth.

Finally, money can be used to keep an account of what you owe if you pay for something a little at a time, or what you owe the government in tax.
