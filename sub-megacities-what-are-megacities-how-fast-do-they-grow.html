How fast does the place where you live change and grow?

One of the most important challenges about megacities is how fast they grow. The number of megacities in the world has grown very quickly in the past 60 years - from only two in 1950 to 23 in 2012.

As the people of the world continue to move into cities and away from small villages, cities will keep growing very quickly. Some megacities, for example Lagos in Nigeria, are growing at about 8% each year exponentially!

In some cities, it is the fast growth of slums that is the main problem. Slums are places of very poor houses overcrowded with people. In some megacities, most people live in slums and new slums can appear in just a few days! This is because large numbers of people move into the city to find work and a home. Most of the growth in cities across the world in the future will happen in poor countries, and mostly in slums.

Exponentially

This means the number of people that the city grows by gets bigger and bigger each year. For a city of ten million people, 8% each year would be like adding 800,000 people in one year; then 864,000 more people the next year; 933,120 more people the year after that, and so on. After just 10 years the population would be over 20 million!
