Why Do We Build Them?

People need to go places, some for business and some travelling on holiday. Because super-fast trains are so fast, can carry so many people and usually travel to and from city centres, they are a popular way to get around.

Super-fast trains can carry about 800 people per train. That's about the same number as the really huge aeroplanes can carry but more people than the more common jet liners.

Travelling by train is also convenient. Some travellers like being able to keep track of their own luggage, and some even work when travelling, which makes super-fast trains ideal. They're also a really good way to see the scenery close up as you travel!

Also, because the trains are powered by electricity, they have the potential to be 'green' and use renewable energy, so this makes them a potentially environmentally friendly way to travel.
