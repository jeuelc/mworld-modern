Rich and Poor Megacities (Lagos & New York)

Megacities are all very different. They grow and work in different ways. Some are very rich, others are really poor. For example, both New York and Lagos are megacities, but they're completely different from each other.

New York is home to some of the world's wealthiest people and the cost of land and housing in New York is very high. Many people in New York work in professional jobs in big companies like lawyers and bankers for example. They live in good houses with running water, electricity and access to schools.

In Lagos, life is very different. Most people are very poor and earn whatever little money they can by selling things that they find or make in markets, or picking things out of rubbish, or working as maids or servants. More than 70% of people in Lagos live in slums where there is often no running water and no toilets. Sometimes kids can't go to school because there are no schools or their parents can't afford for them to go. This is the rich and poor side of different megacities.
