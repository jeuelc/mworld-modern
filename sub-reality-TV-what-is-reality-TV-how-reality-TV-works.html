How Does Reality TV Work?

 
click here

Many reality shows set out to make selected members of the public famous. Most are based on the premise that they will discover ordinary people doing extraordinary things: singing, entertaining, cooking or playing sport.

But often, reality TV participants become celebrities just by being themselves. We seem to like seeing what happens to ordinary people when they suddenly find themselves in the spotlight. Imagine if you woke up one day to find that the whole world knew who you were! Would you enjoy it?

Other reality shows reverse this idea and feature established celebrities being ordinary. The show The Osbournes was popular because it showed that, beneath the fame and fortune, a star such as the famous heavy metal singer Ozzy Osbourne was just like you or me. One famous episode centred on his struggle to figure out how to operate his TV remote! Can you think of some reality TV shows that turn celebrities into 'ordinary' people?

The basic reality format draws our attention to a strange fact: celebrities are people who appear to be 'special' and 'ordinary' at the same time.
