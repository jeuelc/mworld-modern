MagLev Trains!

A smart way to overcome rolling resistance is to have no contact between the wheels and rail! But can you do that without magic? Yes, you sure can!

MagLev, or 'magnetic levitation' technology levitates the train above magnetic rails. You can feel the same thing if you try to push two magnets together with the same poles facing each other.

Not only do MagLev trains and tracks use magnets to keep the train above the track, they also use the magnets to move the train forwards! With magnets the opposite sides attract. So ahead of the train they aim the magnets to line up opposite poles, pulling the train forwards, while behind the train they align the same poles of the magnets to push the train forwards.

All MagLev tracks operate using the same principles, but the location of the magnets; on the train, on the track, or both, and type of magnets; electromagnets, superconducting magnets or permanent magnets, can change depending on the design.
