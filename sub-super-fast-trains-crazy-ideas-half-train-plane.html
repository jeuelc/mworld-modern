A train propelled by... you guessed it! Propellers! In the 1930s someone proposed the Bennie Railplane, with a propeller at the nose of a train attached to rails overhead, but it never got built.

More successful was the Schienenzeppelin or 'Rail Zeppelin' which actually made it to production in Germany in the 1930s. It was able to travel an impressive 230 km/h, with the help of a propeller at the back of the train pushing it.

If the slipstream (the turbulent flow behind a train) is a safety hazard, what do you think about a propeller at the end of a train? This is one of the reasons, as well as engineering reliability, that explains why we don't have propeller plane trains now.
