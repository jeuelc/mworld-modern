Why Put Money In The Bank?

 
click here click here

Like all businesses, banks need to make a profit.

To do this they need to lend money, but to lend money they need to attract new deposits so that they have money to lend. They offer customers like you a little extra money in return for storing your money with them. This is known as interest.

People put their money in the bank to get interest and to keep their wealth safe. If you have savings or cheque account deposits in a bank, you can quickly get access to your money to buy things through electronic payment systems such as EFTPOS and PayPal.

There is even a way that you can earn extra interest! If you put your money in 'term deposits', the bank will pay higher interest to you, but you can't take your money out before the 'term' you've agreed to ends, for example three months. If you do take the money out early, beware the bank charges you a special fee, or 'penalty'.

People put money in the bank to help them save up to buy something substantial or expensive, like a house or a car, or a university fund.... What would you like to save up for?
