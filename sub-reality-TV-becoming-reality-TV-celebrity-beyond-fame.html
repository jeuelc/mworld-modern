Keeping your career going is a concern for all celebrities, whether they have talent or don't. In either case, success depends on an ability to tailor your public image to suit the changing needs of international media industries.

Jackass star Bam Margera is an interesting example of how this works. Margera started life as a professional skateboarder who got into the habit of recording his tricks on a camcorder, just at the time MTV was switching its focus from music to reality television. These videos became the basis for a series of reality prank shows: Jackass, Viva La Bam and Bam's Unholy Union.

All of these shows are about Margera being an ordinary person and having fun with his friends. Ingeniously, however, Margera has been able to sell this same reality format to different industries; film, television, video games and satellite radio. His personality appeals to lots of different types of people, so he can keep selling his shows in different formats.
