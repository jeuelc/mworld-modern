How Can They Go So Fast?

 
click here

Super-fast trains are powered by EMUs! But not the bird emu (although emus can run very fast!); EMU stands for Electric Multiple Units. Each carriage has an electric motor that propels itself along the rails. This makes the train able to reach its super-fast speeds as well as being efficient.

Freight trains and older passenger trains use a locomotive, which is the powered car that pulls a number of unpowered carriages.

Lots of people -- researchers, engineers and designers -- spend a lot of time making sure super-fast trains are able to overcome resistance so that all the power supplied by the EMUs isn't wasted.

Finally, for the trains to reach their maximum speeds, they need smooth rails and flat, straight tracks to run on.
