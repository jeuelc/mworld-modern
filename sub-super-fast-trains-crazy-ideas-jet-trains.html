Jet Trains!

 
click here

Taking it to the next level above propellers, in the 1960s, a rail road engineer attached jet engines to a train! This beast reached just over 290 km/h and achieved a world record for the fastest self-propelled train.

But there was a big 'but'... aside from the obvious safety concerns of strapping jets to the back of a train, because both jet and propeller trains are self-propelled, they need to carry their own fuel. So, they would either have to be bigger so they could store fuel, or they would have to keep on stopping to refuel on long journeys. Carrying the fuel would also mean they'd be heavier and so there would be more resistance.

Sadly, these reasons are why we also don't have jet trains in the modern world.
