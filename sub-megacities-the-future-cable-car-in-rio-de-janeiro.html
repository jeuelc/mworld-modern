Cable Car in Rio de Janeiro

Really big cities can be hard to get around.

The favela of Complexo do Alemao in Rio de Janeiro is built on the side of steep hills on the outskirts of the city. It used to take people as long as two hours to make the journey of three or four kilometres from the top of the hill to the bottom. And sometimes it wasn't possible at all if the public transport wasn't working!

In 2011, moving around in Complexo do Alemao became so much easier when the cable car was built. It provides 152 gondolas taking eight passengers each between six stations through the community.

It takes only 16 minutes to get from the top of the hill to the bottom and is so cheap (residents get two daily rides for free) anyone can use it! This great idea has changed life for everyone in Complexo do Alemao.
