Making Money By Lending

Banks do two really important things in the monetary system: they channel money from savers in the economy to borrowers in the economy, and they create money through lending.Only a small percentage of your deposits are actually kept with the bank. The rest is lent out! Loans made by banks eventually end up as payments to someone for the purchase of goods and services, which that person deposits, magically
creating even more money. This process is called 'money creation'.But sometimes banks lend too much money, or they don't keep enough in reserve. When this happens the bank might not be able to pay back all the people that lent it money in the first place. The bank can go bankrupt. When lots of banks do this, a whole country can go bankrupt! This has happened, or almost happened, several times in the last ten years.
