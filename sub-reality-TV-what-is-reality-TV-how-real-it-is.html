How Real Is It?

 
click here click here

Most people know that reality TV isn't real. The people you see on it are selected from thousands of others because they suit certain roles. For example, a singer on a talent contest might be a good contestant because, aside from their singing talent, they also have an interesting life story.

Even fictional characters have to be 'real'. Think about action heroes like Dwayne Johnson (The Rock). The characters he plays in movies have to match what we know about the 'real' Dwayne; his history as an athlete and professional wrestler.

The 'fictional' and 'real' parts all become Johnson's celebrity image. This is what attracts us to him.

The people who make TV entertainment know it works best if viewers care about the characters on screen. They do this by carefully building ideas about what this person is like. It isn't about being true or real. It's about being believable.
