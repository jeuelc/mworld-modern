The Look

Looks matter for all celebrities, but is it always about 'looking good'?

You don't have to be traditionally 'pretty' or 'handsome' to succeed, but your look must match your celebrity story. Susan Boyle, from Britain's Got Talent, wasn't young like most pop stars, but this turned out to be a perfect fit for her and the show. At first, people were surprised that someone without a traditional pop star look could sing so well. This underlined the dangers of judging on appearances, and got a lot of people talking. It also helped to make the show popular. Susans success on Britain's Got Talent showed that fame can come to people of any age or style.

Lots of celebrities understand this, even when they are just starting out! In 2008, Australian teenager Corey Delaney became internationally famous for throwing a house party that got out of hand. Interviewed about the incident on TV, Corey refused to remove a pair of yellow-framed sunglasses 'because theyre famous'. What he meant was that the glasses were a part of his celebrity look. Those glasses became a big part of Coreys 'brand', and helped him to be recognisable in a crowd. But where is Corey now? Did his fame last?
