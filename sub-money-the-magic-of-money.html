The Magic Of Money

All coin and paper money is known as 'currency'. The majority of money, though, is made up of 'deposits' at banks, which are electronic entries that show up on bank statements.

People put their money in banks to earn interest. But it doesn't just sit there gathering dust it gets put to work! Banks lend out a certain fraction of those deposits to borrowers at higher interest and keep the rest as reserves. (Bankers call this 'fractional reserve banking'.) This difference between interest paid by banks on deposits and higher interest received by banks from borrowers becomes bank profit. So by lending money, banks create more money!

It's important that banks keep some deposits in reserve, to give back to their customers when they need it. If banks don't have enough money to pay back their customers they might go bankrupt!

The supply of money is controlled by the 'central bank' (run by the government). This bank can buy or sell 'government bonds' or they can tell the mint to print more coins or notes.

Government Bonds

Government bonds are special IOUs that you can buy from your government. They say that the government owes you however much money is on the bond.
