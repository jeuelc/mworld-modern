Super Fast Trains

Do you like the idea of zooming across the countryside at up to 500 km/h? What about speeding as fast as that while you're just metres from the ground, or even floating above it thanks to magnets?

Super-fast trains can move people from place to place over great distances safely, efficiently and quickly.

But how do they do it? What makes them special? What are the things we have to worry about when were going so fast? Engineers have to overcome resistance, especially from the air, to get trains to the super-fast travelling speeds, and they also have to worry about wind from the trains blowing people over, and even sonic booms!

And what about the future of transport? Vacuum tubes, flying trains... what next?
