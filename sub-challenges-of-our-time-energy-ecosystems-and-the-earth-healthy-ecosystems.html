An ecosystem is a web of living things and the environment they live in. It's a web because everything is connected in some way to everything else.
Plants provide food for herbivores. Herbivores can spread seeds for the next generation of plants through their poo or fur. Predators like lions and tigers eat herbivores. When predators and other animals die, their bodies are eaten by bugs which create nutrients for the soil, from which more plants grow.
The Earth's environment is always changing, for example getting hotter or colder. Ecosystems change over time as the Earth's environment changes. But human activity such as the building of cities and the harvesting of water, trees and animals from ecosystems is accelerating the speed of change. Many ecosystems cannot cope with the speed of change. The web breaks down, and the ecosystem no longer supports a healthy, natural environment.

Herbivores

Herbivores are animals that eat plants, such as grasses and leaves. Herbivores include beasts such as cows, sheep, gazelles, zebras, elephants, giraffes, rhinos, kangaroos, koalas, pandas and many more!
