Imagine watching a TV show about factory workers that showed an entire day from start to finish. You learn about how the machines work, what the workers do and what the factory makes. Does it sound interesting? Now imagine that in that day a worker got a promotion, and maybe two other workers had an argument and wouldn't work together again. Would you want to watch that show?

It isn't enough that reality TV tells you about the world; it has to do it in an engaging way. If it can keep your interest, reality TV can teach you about some really important issues like gender, nutrition, race or identity. In particular, the characters have to be people that you care about and that sustain your interest.

And there has to be a story that viewers can become involved in, or you wont want to watch again!

Reality TV features conflict and competition. Real people are there to play roles that support entertaining stories in the show. By turning real people into characters, this makes them seem more real to viewers. Whether we love these characters, or love to hate them, we engage with them, which is what brings us back to watch the show each week.
