Every place in the world is unique, and so are megacities. The one thing they all have in common is that they are REALLY big. They are also usually very dense, which means many people live in a small area.

But megacities are not just bigger and denser, they are also different from other kinds of cities. Often, megacities don't really have one place called the city centre. Can you imagine a city so big and so busy that you might not be able to travel across it in one day?

That means lots of different places have to serve people with jobs, shops, hospitals, and schools. Some parts of megacities are very modern and wealthy, like the centre of New York or parts of Sao Paulo. But megacities also have high numbers of very poor people, and many have to live in homes that they built themselves, sometimes out of nothing more than cardboard and tin, because there is no proper housing. These people are sometimes called 'squatters' and often live in very poor conditions where there is no running water, no toilets, and no paved roads.
