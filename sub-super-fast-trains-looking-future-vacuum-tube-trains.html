Vacuum Tube Trains

 
click here click here

People are already talking about vacuum, or 'reduced pressure', tubes as a way to travel in the future. Some science fiction movies show similar kinds of transport. If you reduced the air in a tube, or took it out altogether, then there'd be little or no aerodynamic resistance.

Without aerodynamic resistance trains could move more efficiently and much faster. In a vacuum, a train could travel even faster than a plane! And it wouldn't matter if the train went faster than the speed of sound, because there would be no air to cause a sonic boom.

But looking after a vacuum tube would be a nightmare. Even a tiny hole in the tube would let air in and mess up the system. How could you make sure there was never any earthquake damage, or manufacturing faults? It would be really difficult!

You would have to think about safety too, with the speed at which the train could travel. A system like this would likely cost a lot too, even more than fast trains, which aren't cheap!
