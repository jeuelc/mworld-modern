Big Brother is the classic example of a show about people who seem ordinary who become celebrities by doing ordinary things. The show assembles a cast, confines its members to a giant film set fashioned like a house, then films them cooking, eating, sleeping and socializing.

Big Brother shows us how television can make reality. Although it isn't scripted, the program guarantees conflict, drama and entertainment. Because a range of personality types are chosen, clashes between cast members are inevitable. Shows like Big Brother are also carefully edited we can't see every minute of the contestants lives, and scenes are carefully chosen to tell an entertaining story.

The program doesn't show ordinary life, it transforms ordinary life into a media event. It changes people who seem ordinary into heroes and villains just like a soap opera. Viewers start to love or hate these people, stories about them spill over into the popular press, talk shows, the internet and even political debate.

The famous 'eviction nights' become highly anticipated live events; just like a sporting final. At the end, we know the winner will be a celebrity, even if it's only for a while.
