Throughout history, money has mostly been in the form of metal coins such as gold, silver and bronze. The metals themselves had value, so these coins were known as 'commodity money'. Anything with value can be commodity money, but it is a better idea to put your wealth in metals which would last, rather than in cattle or tobacco, which would be pretty hard to store and would perish over time!

In the 19th and 20th centuries, some countries used 'representative money', a piece of paper that represented the value of metal. You could exchange paper notes for gold at a fixed price. This was called the 'Gold Standard'.

Today, we have what's known as 'fiat' money: its value is assigned by government. 'Fiat' is Latin for 'let it be done!' Fiat money lasts a long time and its easy to store, especially in the form of electronic deposits as shown on a bank statement. This encourages society to build up and store wealth over time.
