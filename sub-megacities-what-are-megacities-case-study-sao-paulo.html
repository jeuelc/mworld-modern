Case Study: Sao Paulo

Sao Paulo is Brazil's biggest city and the eight largest in the world with about 20 million people.

Around 20% of those people live in squatter settlements that in Latin America are called 'favelas'. Most of these are on the very edge of the city, and that is where this megacity has grown the fastest.

Many people are poor and don't have secure places to live. Yet Sao Paulo is also home to very wealthy people and it's the main centre for business in Brazil. It is a very modern city, especially in the wealthy areas. The land and house prices in Sao Paulo are very high, making this megacity one of the 10 most expensive in the world! This means that Sao Paulo is very unequal: there's a huge difference between the lives of the very rich and the very poor.
