Growing Food in the City

Millions of people living in slums never know if they'll have food the next day, and spend most of their tiny incomes on food. New projects to try and ensure people have access to cheap, nutritious and local food have started up in slum areas all around the world.

One project is the idea of growing fruit and vegetables in containers anywhere there's space - in tiny backyards, on balconies, on the rooftops, even on carts in the street! These are sometimes called 'vertical gardens'. This has given thousands of people in the slums of cities like Mexico City and Kibera in Nairobi access to cheap, fresh food.

The main problem was how to fertilise the soil in containers without using expensive and harmful chemicals. The solution? Wee! Urine was found to be the best fertiliser for containers of fruits and vegetables and these projects are now helping families everywhere around the city!
