Before money was created, people traded things with each other by offering what they had for what they wanted. You might swap your goat for a whole heap of grain, for example. This was called a 'barter' system. It wasn't very efficient because you had to find someone who had exactly what you wanted and wanted whatever you had, at the same place and at the same time! This is known as a 'double coincidence of wants', and it means people waste a lot of time searching for the right person to trade with. You also need exact amounts; it doesn't allow for change to be left over.

Some people still use a barter system today, but money is much more common because it reduces the time spent searching and means you can pay for anything at any place at any time with anyone. Money also lets you give change if the exact amount isn't paid because the value of one item is greater than the other.
