How Much Money Is There?

Money is made up of notes, coins and deposits in banks. The amount of money out there in the economy is hard to work out because there are many different types of deposits and therefore different definitions of money.

Every country has a 'central bank' run by the government that puts money into the economy and takes it out. They don't stand in the street handing out coins though! It works like this. Central banks increase deposits of money in the economy by buying bits of paper from the public called 'government bonds'. A government bond is basically an IOU stating: 'the government owes you X dollars'.

On the other hand, the central bank can sell these bits of paper to the public, which reduces banks deposit and therefore the amount of money in the economy.

Central banks can also put more money into the economy by printing more money, but if they print too much money it can cause inflation!
